One Arm Handstand

Expert

The one-arm handstand is one of the most impressive calisthenics skills, requiring extraordinary balance, core tension, and unilateral strength. In this variation, the practitioner balances vertically using only one arm, with the other arm typically held against the torso or extended to the side for balance. The supporting arm must bear the entire body weight, demanding tremendous strength in the shoulder girdle and stabilizing muscles. Proper technique involves slight body angulation over the supporting hand and intense core engagement to prevent rotation.

Muscles Worked:

Primary (supporting side): Deltoids, Triceps, Serratus Anterior, Rotator Cuff

Secondary: Core (obliques, transverse abdominis - for anti-rotation), Pectorals, Forearms

Stabilizers: Spinal Erectors, Glutes, Quadriceps, Non-supporting arm (isometric hold)
