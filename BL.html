Back Lever

Advanced

The back lever is an intermediate-to-advanced calisthenics skill performed on a pull-up bar where the body is held face-down and parallel to the ground with arms straight. This static hold requires good back, core, and shoulder strength. The body forms a straight line from hands to feet, with shoulders in a protracted position. It's often used as a progression toward more advanced skills like the iron cross or as a complementary exercise to the front lever.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Posterior Deltoids, Trapezius, Rhomboids, Spinal Erectors

Secondary: Core (especially lower back), Forearms, Triceps

Stabilizers: Infraspinatus, Teres Minor, Wrist Flexors, Glutes
