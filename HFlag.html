Human Flag

Expert

The human flag is an iconic calisthenics skill where the body is held horizontally from a vertical pole, resembling a flag waving in the wind. The practitioner supports their weight with one arm pressing against the pole at shoulder level and the other arm gripping the pole at waist level, with legs extended horizontally. This extreme static hold requires exceptional core strength, grip strength, and coordination between both sides of the body. It's one of the most visually impressive calisthenics feats.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Obliques, Quadratus Lumborum, Latissimus Dorsi, Core (anti-rotational)

Secondary: Grip muscles, Shoulder stabilizers, Adductors, Abductors

Stabilizers: Transverse Abdominis, Spinal Erectors, Glutes, Quadriceps
