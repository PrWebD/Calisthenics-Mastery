Back Lever Raise

Advanced

The back lever raise is a dynamic progression of the static back lever, where the practitioner lifts their body from a hanging position into a full back lever. This movement builds strength in the posterior chain and shoulder stabilizers. Proper form involves initiating the movement with the rear delts and upper back, maintaining a straight body line, and controlling the descent. This exercise develops the strength needed for advanced static holds and transitions.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Posterior Deltoids, Trapezius, Rhomboids, Spinal Erectors

Secondary: Core (posterior chain), Forearms, Triceps

Stabilizers: Infraspinatus, Teres Minor, Wrist Flexors, Glutes
