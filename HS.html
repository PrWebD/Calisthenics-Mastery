Handstand

Advanced

The handstand is a fundamental calisthenics skill where the practitioner balances on their hands with their body in a vertical position. This static hold requires significant shoulder strength, core stability, and balance. Proper handstand technique involves straight arms, engaged shoulders (shoulder blades protracted), tight core, and legs together with toes pointed. It serves as the foundation for many advanced skills like handstand push-ups and one-arm variations.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Deltoids (shoulders), Triceps, Trapezius, Serratus Anterior

Secondary: Core (abdominals, obliques), Forearms, Pectorals (chest)

Stabilizers: Rotator cuff, Glutes, Quadriceps
