Back Lever Pull

Expert

The back lever pull is an advanced dynamic skill that combines the back lever position with a pulling motion. Starting from a back lever, the practitioner pulls themselves upward while maintaining the horizontal body position. This movement requires not only the strength for a static back lever but also controlled pulling power. It develops exceptional strength in the posterior chain and serves as preparation for more complex transitions and skills.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Posterior Deltoids, Trapezius, Rhomboids, Spinal Erectors

Secondary: Core (posterior chain), Forearms, Triceps

Stabilizers: Infraspinatus, Teres Minor, Wrist Flexors, Glutes
