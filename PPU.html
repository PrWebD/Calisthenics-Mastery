Planche Push-Up

Expert

The planche push-up is a dynamic progression of the static planche, where the practitioner lowers their body while maintaining the planche body position and pushes back up. This movement dramatically increases the strength requirements of the anterior delts, chest, and serratus anterior. Proper form requires maintaining a perfectly straight body line throughout the movement, with controlled descent and powerful pressing. This skill represents one of the highest levels of upper body pushing strength in calisthenics.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Anterior Deltoids, Pectorals, Triceps, Serratus Anterior

Secondary: Core (for maintaining body alignment), Forearms, Wrist Flexors

Stabilizers: Coracobrachialis, Transverse Abdominis, Hip Flexors
