Front Lever Raise

Expert

The front lever raise is a dynamic progression from the static front lever, where the practitioner lifts their body from a hanging position into a full front lever. This movement emphasizes the concentric (lifting) phase of the skill, building strength for the static hold. Proper form involves initiating the movement with the lats, maintaining a straight body line, and controlling the descent. This exercise is excellent for developing the pulling strength needed for advanced calisthenics.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Latissimus Dorsi, Biceps, Rhomboids, Teres Major

Secondary: Core (rectus abdominis), Forearms, Rear Deltoids

Stabilizers: Trapezius, Infraspinatus, Wrist Flexors, Hip Flexors
