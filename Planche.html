Planche

Expert

The planche is an advanced calisthenics skill where the body is held parallel to the ground, supported only by the hands, with arms straight and legs elevated. This extreme static hold requires exceptional anterior delts, chest, and core strength, as well as wrist flexibility. The body forms a straight line from hands to feet, with shoulders in an extreme protracted position. Progressions typically start with tuck planche, then advance to straddle and finally full planche.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Anterior Deltoids, Pectorals, Serratus Anterior, Coracobrachialis

Secondary: Core (rectus abdominis, obliques), Wrist Flexors, Triceps

Stabilizers: Forearms, Transverse Abdominis, Hip Flexors, Quadriceps
