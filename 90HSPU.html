90° Handstand Push-Up

Expert

The 90° handstand push-up (also known as a bent-arm handstand or elbow lever push-up) is an extremely advanced variation where the practitioner balances in a handstand with elbows bent at 90 degrees, then performs push-ups from this position. This skill requires exceptional shoulder, triceps, and core strength, as well as precise balance control. The movement path is much shorter than a full handstand push-up, but the static hold at 90 degrees places immense strain on the supporting muscles. This skill bridges the gap between handstand and planche progressions.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Deltoids, Triceps, Pectorals, Serratus Anterior

Secondary: Forearms, Core (abdominals, obliques), Trapezius

Stabilizers: Rotator cuff, Spinal Erectors, Glutes, Quadriceps
