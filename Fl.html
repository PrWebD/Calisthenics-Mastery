Front Lever

Expert

The front lever is an advanced calisthenics skill performed on a pull-up bar where the body is held horizontal to the ground with arms straight and palms facing the body. This static hold requires exceptional back, core, and grip strength. The body forms a straight line from hands to feet, with shoulders in a depressed position. Progressions typically start with tuck front lever, then advance to advanced tuck, straddle, and finally full front lever.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Latissimus Dorsi, Biceps, Rhomboids, Teres Major

Secondary: Core (especially lower abs), Forearms, Rear Deltoids

Stabilizers: Trapezius, Infraspinatus, Wrist Flexors, Hip Flexors
