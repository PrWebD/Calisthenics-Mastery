One Arm Front Lever

Expert

The one-arm front lever is among the most difficult calisthenics skills, requiring extraordinary unilateral back strength and body control. In this variation, the body is held horizontal to the ground, supported by a single arm on the bar. The demands on the supporting latissimus dorsi, biceps, and core are immense, requiring not only strength but also precise balance and anti-rotational control. The non-supporting arm is typically extended to the side for balance. This skill represents the pinnacle of pulling strength in calisthenics.

Muscles Worked:

Primary (supporting side): Latissimus Dorsi, Biceps, Rhomboids, Teres Major

Secondary: Core (obliques, transverse abdominis - for anti-rotation), Forearms

Stabilizers: Trapezius, Infraspinatus, Wrist Flexors, Hip Flexors, Non-supporting arm
