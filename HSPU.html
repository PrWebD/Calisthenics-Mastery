Handstand Push-Up

Expert

The handstand push-up is a dynamic progression from the static handstand, requiring the practitioner to lower their head toward the ground and push back up to a full handstand position. This movement dramatically increases the demand on shoulder and triceps strength. Proper form involves maintaining a straight body line throughout the movement, controlling the descent, and pressing through the palms to return to the top position. Wall-assisted variations are commonly used for training and building strength.

Muscles Worked:

Primary: Deltoids (especially anterior), Triceps, Upper Pectorals, Serratus Anterior

Secondary: Trapezius, Core (for stabilization), Forearms

Stabilizers: Rotator cuff, Spinal Erectors, Glutes
